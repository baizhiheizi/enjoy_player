Note on the rounding tests: Dart's `Duration(milliseconds: (3.5 * 1000).round()) == 3500ms` and `Duration.inSeconds` truncates the float-second back to 3, so the visible output is `00:03` for 3.4/3.5/3.6. The .round() in the helper pins the *policy* (always round before constructing Duration) so a future `int milliseconds` site can't accidentally truncate `(seconds * 1000).toInt()` and show 00:00 for 2.7s audio. The detector's "fractional second divergence" claim is the wrong framing ‚Äî the real risk is policy drift between the 6 sites that hand-write the conversion.
